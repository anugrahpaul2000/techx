Types of Fuel and Combustion Process

By Anugrah Vishwas Paul

02 August 2020



Hey Guys! If you're a Petrolhead like me, you must get excited at the sound of a Nicely Reving Smooth Engine. But do you know how a metal turns Liquid Fuel into Noise and Power? Not to Worry!
Broadly used Fuel in a Automobile is Petrol or Diesel. We'll be considering 4-Stroke Engine for this explanation. Detailed Explanation of 4-Stroke & 2-Stroke Engine is coming soon as well as the comparison among them.

As the name suggests 4 Stroke consists of:

  1. Intake:

    2. The First stroke in which the piston moves down from the Top-Dead-Center to the Bottom-Dead-Center. Effectively creating a Vaccum for the Air to enter the Cylinder. Since the Basic Recipe for a Fire is Air (Oxygen) and Fuel.

    a) Petrol:

    The Fuel enters the cylinder pre-mixed with the air i.e., (Carburetor) or is injected directly into the cylinder through Fuel Injectors.

    b) Diesel:

    Fuel is not mixed in the Intake Stroke.

  2. Compression

    3. The Second Stroke, in this the piston moves from the Bottom-Dead-Center to the Top-Dead-Center. Since the Piston is moving up, it compresses the Air/Fuel Mixture inside it making it extremely HOT!

    a) Petrol:

    A Spark Plug is Fitted on the Top-Dead-Center of the Cylinder creates a Spark causing the Air-Fuel Mixture to Ignite.

    b) Diesel:

    Here no spark plug is available the piston compresses the air making it extremely Hot (As there's no Spark, Piston can Move further up), Just before the Piston reaches the Top. Fuel is injected in the extremely Hot Air, causing it to combust immdeiately.

  3. Power:

    4. The Combustion/Explosion causes the air to expand which forces the Piston on its down way back to the Bottom-Dead-Center. Since it happens due to an explosion inside the Cylinder. Hence it's known as Power Stroke. It's the same for both Petrol as well as Diesel

  4. Exhaust

    5. In this stroke the Piston is on the way Back up all the while pushing the Smoke and spent Fuel/Air out of the Cylinder. As it gets ready for the next 4-Stroke Cycle.
