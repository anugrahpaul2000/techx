I know you are excited to create your first filter and brag it between your friend circle( :D I know). But before starting let me tell you what will be the teaching pattern of our whole series. So instead of understanding the interface in a one go we'll learn about it while making our filters.(ComeOn! I don't want to bore you). So let's start.

Fire Up! I mean launch SparkAR on your System.It should look something like as shown below.(Note: With different version the screen may vary)



Come back here don't get tempted with awesome templates.

Let's make our own

Click on Blank Project and a new screen will pop-up in front of you. Something like as shown below.



So many options but don't be overwhelmed.Follow the steps as I mentioned below.

  • Step 1 Download Face Assets Click Here!
    • For now just extract the downloaded zip and save it somewhere on your system.

  • Step 2 Create a facetracker and facemesh. As shown below
    • Scene Panel->Add Object ->Face Tracker
    Scene Panel->Add Object ->Face Mesh


    Make sure faceMesh0 is child of faceTracker0.Now Let's learn what we just did. So faceTracker0 is helping us to track the movement of the face while the faceMesh0 overlays a mask( that chess board kind of pattern. It's basically a 3d face mask ) and since it's a child of faceTracker0 therefore it's position changes with movement of face.

  • Step 3 Assign Material and texture. As show below
    • Select faceMesh0 -> Properies Panel -> + Material
    Select Material -> Properties Panel -> Texture -> Choose texture file


    So what we just did is assigned a material and on that material we assigned the texture( FaceMask tracker file inside the FaceAssets bundle we downloaded in Step1 ).
    Now let's understand the working. A material defines how a 3D object will react to the environment light and camera (i.e. properties of 3d model like roughness, metallic behaviour, transparency etc). While texture is given to material to assign favourable color/pattern to the 3D object. So here we assigned material to FaceMesh(3D Model) and then assigned Facetracker.png as texture to material.
